**Last updated:** 10 June 2026
**Applies to:** the ArkenEdu web application at https://arkenedu.com, the ArkenEdu mobile application (distributed on Google Play and the Apple App Store), and all related application programming interfaces, back-end services, and communications operated by ArkenEdu.

## 1. Introduction

ArkenEdu ("**ArkenEdu**", "**we**", "**us**", or "**our**") provides a School Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) and school-management platform (the "**Platform**") to private schools, K–12 institutions, and other educational institutions in India (each, a "**School**"). The Platform helps Schools manage admissions, student records, attendance, examinations and report cards, academic records, timetables, homework and assignments, communication and announcements, fee and finance management, document storage, and related operations, and provides web and mobile access for school owners, principals, administrators, teachers, staff, students, and parents.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data the Platform processes, how and why it is processed, who it is shared with, how long it is kept, how it is secured, and the rights available to the individuals whose data we handle.

### 1.1 Our role: who controls your data

Almost all of the personal data on the Platform is uploaded, generated, or entered by a School and by the users the School authorises. In relation to that data:

- **The School is the Data Fiduciary / Data Controller.** The School decides which records to maintain, who may access them, and the purposes for which they are used. The School is responsible for collecting any consent required from students, parents, staff, and others, and for the lawfulness of the instructions it gives us.
- **ArkenEdu is the Data Processor.** We process personal data **on behalf of and under the documented instructions of the School**, principally to operate, maintain, secure, and support the Platform. Our processing of School data is governed by our [Data Processing Agreement](data-processing-agreement.md) ("**DPA**") with each School.

For a limited set of data — for example, the contact and billing details of the individuals who administer a School's subscription, and Platform-operations data such as security logs — ArkenEdu acts as a **Data Fiduciary / Controller** in its own right. This Policy identifies where that is the case.

If you are a student, parent, teacher, or member of staff and you have questions about how your School uses your data, please contact your School in the first instance. If you have a question about how ArkenEdu processes data as a processor, contact us using the details in Section 18.

### 1.2 Laws we work under

We design the Platform to support Schools in meeting their obligations under, and we process personal data in accordance with:

- the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)** and rules made under it;
- the **Information Technology Act, 2000**, and the **Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 (SPDI Rules)**; and
- other applicable Indian laws governing the records that Schools are required to maintain.

Where the Platform is accessed from outside India, additional local laws may apply to the relevant School; Schools remain responsible for their own compliance.

## 3. Who the Platform is for, and our policy on children

The Platform is an administrative tool used by Schools and the users a School authorises. It is **not** directed to the general public and is **not** an ad-supported consumer service.

Many users of the Platform are children (students under the age of 18). We treat student data with heightened care:

- Student accounts and records are created and managed by the School, not by children signing up independently. We do not knowingly establish a direct, consumer-style relationship with a child outside the School context.
- Where the law requires verifiable consent of a parent or lawful guardian for processing a child's personal data, **the School (as Data Fiduciary) is responsible for obtaining and recording that consent** before the child's data is entered into the Platform.
- Consistent with the DPDP Act, we do **not** undertake tracking, behavioural monitoring, or targeted advertising directed at children, and we do not process children's personal data in a way that is likely to cause a detrimental effect on their well-being.
- In many Schools, a parent accesses the Platform using the student's login (see Section 4.2). Where this is the case, the parent and student share the same account, and data visible in that account is governed by the School's configuration.

If you believe a child's data has been provided to the Platform without the consent required by law, contact us (Section 18) and we will work with the relevant School to address it.

## 4. The accounts and identities on the Platform

### 4.1 Account provisioning

Accounts on the Platform are **provisioned by the School**, not by self-service public sign-up. A School administrator creates accounts for principals, administrators, teachers, staff, students, and (where applicable) parents, and assigns each account a role. Access to data is governed by **role-based access control (RBAC)** — what a user can see and do is determined by the role the School assigns.

### 4.2 The shared parent/student login model

For many Schools, ArkenEdu operates a model in which **a parent accesses the Platform using their child's student login**, rather than holding a separate parent account. In these cases:

- the parent and the student use the same set of credentials and see the same account;
- a separate "parent" record may not exist for that user; and
- the data shown reflects the student's records as configured by the School.

Where a School does provision distinct parent accounts, those accounts are treated as described for parents below. Because of this model, references in this Policy to "parent data" and "student data" may relate to the same shared account.

## 5. Personal data we process

The categories below describe data processed through the Platform. Most of it is provided by the School or its users; some is generated automatically when the Platform is used. **The specific fields maintained for any given individual are configured by the School.**

### 5.1 Student data
- **Identity and profile:** name, photograph, date of birth, gender, student/admission/roll number, class and section, house, and academic year/enrolment records.
- **Family and contact links:** linkage to parent/guardian contact details, address, and emergency contacts as recorded by the School.
- **Attendance:** daily/period attendance status, absences, and related notes.
- **Academic and examination data:** marks, grades, assessment results, report cards, mastery/analytics derived from results, subject and topic performance, homework and assignment submissions and status.
- **Timetable and activities:** class schedule and related academic scheduling.
- **Communications:** announcements, messages, and notifications addressed to the student/parent account, and records that such communications were sent.
- **Documents:** files uploaded against the student record by the School (for example certificates or admission documents), and media stored for the School.
- **Financial:** fee structure assigned to the student, invoices, dues/arrears (including arrears carried over between academic years), payment status, and payment references (such as a UPI transaction reference / UTR) submitted by a parent and any associated proof-of-payment file.

### 5.2 Parent / guardian data
- Name, relationship to the student, phone number(s), email address, postal address, and emergency-contact details, as recorded by the School.
- Where a parent uses the shared student login (Section 4.2), the parent's activity is associated with that account.
- Fee-payment information submitted through the parent portal, including the payment reference and any uploaded proof of payment.
- Communications and notification preferences and delivery records.

### 5.3 Teacher and staff data
- Identity and profile: name, photograph, employee identifier, role/designation, subjects and classes assigned, and contact details (email, phone).
- Account credentials and authentication data (Section 7).
- Work product entered into the Platform: attendance marked, marks and assessments entered, homework and lesson content created, announcements authored, documents uploaded, and academic content submitted to the Platform's AI-assisted tools (Section 11.2).
- Activity and audit records associated with the actions a staff member performs in the Platform.

### 5.4 School owner / principal / administrator data
- Name, role, official email address and phone number, and the School(s) and scope each administrator is authorised to manage.
- Where the individual administers the School's relationship with ArkenEdu (for example the subscription or billing contact), we also process their contact and billing-administration details **as a Data Fiduciary in our own right** to manage our service relationship with the School.

### 5.5 Data generated automatically when the Platform is used
- **Authentication and session data:** session identifiers and tokens used to keep you signed in and to enforce role-based access (Section 7).
- **Device and technical data:** IP address, browser type and version, operating-system and device type, app version, language, and time-zone, and — for the mobile app — device model and OS version collected to register and deliver push notifications (Section 8).
- **Push-notification identifiers:** the push token issued for your device so we can deliver notifications.
- **Logs and diagnostics:** server access logs, application logs, and error/diagnostic events (Section 9), including the error-tracking data described in Section 11.
- **Usage data:** in-product activity needed to operate features (for example which records were viewed or changed), and operational metrics. We do **not** operate third-party advertising or behavioural-analytics SDKs.

### 5.6 Sensitive personal data
Some of the above may constitute "sensitive personal data or information" under the SPDI Rules — for example passwords and financial information (such as payment references). We apply heightened safeguards to such data (Section 10). The Platform is **not** designed to be a repository for health records, biometric data, or government identity numbers; Schools should avoid uploading such data unless their own legal basis and configuration require it, and remain responsible for any such data they choose to store.

## 6. Why we process personal data (purposes and legal basis)

As a **Data Processor**, we process School data only to provide and support the Platform under the School's instructions, including to:

1. create and administer accounts and enforce role-based access;
2. operate the modules the School uses (admissions, attendance, exams and report cards, academic records, timetable, homework, communication, finance, document storage, analytics, and reporting);
3. deliver communications, announcements, and push notifications the School sends;
4. generate AI-assisted academic materials when a teacher uses those tools (Section 11.2);
5. secure the Platform, prevent and investigate abuse, and maintain audit trails;
6. provide technical support and resolve incidents;
7. back up, restore, and maintain the integrity of data; and
8. meet legal and regulatory obligations that apply to us as a processor.

The **legal basis** for processing School data — including any consent required from students, parents, and staff, and any verifiable parental consent for children's data — is established and maintained by the **School as Data Fiduciary**. Where ArkenEdu acts as a Data Fiduciary (Section 4.1 / 5.4), our basis is the performance of our service contract with the School, our legitimate interest in operating and securing the Platform, and compliance with law.

We do **not** use School data to build advertising profiles, and we do **not** sell personal data.

## 7. Authentication and session management

- **Credentials.** Accounts are authenticated with the credentials issued or set through the School. Passwords are stored using salted one-way hashing; we do not store plaintext passwords.
- **Web sessions.** On the web application, sessions are maintained using **HttpOnly cookies** that are keyed to the user's role and used to enforce role-based access control. Authorisation decisions are derived from signed session claims. These cookies are strictly necessary for the service to function (see Section 16).
- **Transport security.** All traffic between your device and the Platform is encrypted in transit over HTTPS/TLS.
- **Mobile sessions.** In the mobile app, authentication tokens are stored on the device using the operating system's secure storage (via Expo SecureStore — Keychain on iOS, Keystore-backed storage on Android).
- **Session handling.** Sessions expire after a period of inactivity or on sign-out. Server-side session and cache state may be held transiently in Redis to operate the service.

## 8. Mobile application — permissions and notifications

The ArkenEdu mobile application (distributed under the app identifier `com.arkenedu.mobile`) requests only the device capabilities it needs to function:

- **Notifications permission.** Used to deliver push notifications about school activity (for example announcements, attendance, fee reminders, and results). You may decline or later disable this permission in your device settings; the rest of the app continues to work, but you will not receive push notifications.
- **Network/Internet access.** Required to communicate securely with ArkenEdu's servers.
- **Secure device storage.** Used to store your authentication session securely on the device (Section 7); this does not require a user-facing permission prompt.

The current mobile application does **not** request camera, photo-library, microphone, contacts, location, or general file-storage permissions. Where the mobile app surfaces a flow that requires uploading a file (for example submitting proof of a fee payment), it directs you to the secure web portal, where the upload takes place.

### 8.1 Push notifications

Push notifications are delivered through the **Expo push-notification service**, which in turn uses the platform notification gateways (Apple Push Notification service for iOS and Firebase Cloud Messaging for Android) to reach your device. To register your device for notifications we process a **push token** and basic device information (such as device model and OS version) via the Expo Device library. We use this only to deliver Platform notifications; we do not use push tokens for advertising. You can turn notifications off at any time in your device settings.

## 9. Analytics, logs, and diagnostics

- **In-product analytics.** Features such as "mastery analytics" and academic reports are computed from the School's own academic data to help Schools, teachers, and parents understand performance. This is part of the Platform's functionality, not third-party tracking.
- **Operational logs.** We keep server access logs and application logs (including IP address, timestamps, request metadata, and error information) to operate, secure, debug, and audit the Platform.
- **Error and crash diagnostics.** We use **Sentry** to capture application errors and diagnostics so we can detect and fix problems. Our configuration is oriented to error reporting (performance tracing is disabled or sampled at zero by default). Diagnostic events may include technical context and limited identifiers needed to investigate the error.
- **No advertising analytics.** We do not integrate advertising, attribution, or behavioural-analytics SDKs, and we do not build advertising profiles of any user, including children.

## 10. How we protect data (security measures)

We implement reasonable security practices and procedures designed to protect personal data, including:

- **Encryption in transit** using HTTPS/TLS for all client-server communication, and encryption at rest for stored data and backups on our hosting provider.
- **Role-based access control** so users — and ArkenEdu personnel — can access only the data appropriate to their role; administrative access to production systems is restricted on a least-privilege, need-to-know basis.
- **Secure credential handling**, including salted password hashing and secure session/token management (Section 7).
- **Time-limited, signed access** to stored files. Documents and media held in object storage are served through short-lived pre-signed URLs (which expire, by default, within one hour) rather than being publicly listable.
- **Network and platform hardening**, logging, monitoring, and error tracking to detect and respond to issues.
- **Backups** to support recovery and continuity.
- **Vendor controls**, including contractual confidentiality and security commitments from our sub-processors (Section 11).

No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure. While we work to protect personal data using the measures above, we cannot guarantee absolute security. Schools and users share responsibility for security — for example by safeguarding credentials, managing role assignments responsibly, and promptly reporting suspected compromise.

## 11. Third-party service providers (sub-processors)

We use a limited set of vendors to operate the Platform. These vendors process data **only to provide their service to us** under contractual confidentiality, security, and data-protection terms, and are not permitted to use the data for their own purposes. The current sub-processors are:

### 11.1 Infrastructure and storage
- **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** — cloud hosting and compute (the application runs on AWS EC2) and object storage (**Amazon S3**) for uploaded documents, media, announcement attachments, teacher file libraries, and proof-of-payment files. See Section 12.
- **Cloudinary** *(legacy)* — historically used for media hosting. Some older records may still reference Cloudinary-hosted media URLs; new uploads are stored on AWS S3.

### 11.2 AI-assisted academic tools
- **OpenAI** and **Google (Generative AI / Gemini)** — used by the optional Question Bank and Lesson Plan generation features. When a teacher uses these tools, the academic content they submit (such as chapter or syllabus text) is sent to the AI provider to generate questions or lesson plans. These tools are intended for **teacher-created academic content** and should not be used to submit student personal data. We use these providers under API terms configured so that submitted content is not used by the provider to train its general models.

### 11.3 Communications
- **Expo** (push-notification delivery), which routes through **Apple Push Notification service** and **Firebase Cloud Messaging** (Section 8.1).
- **Twilio** — used for outbound voice communications (for example automated voice calls to parents). To place a call, the recipient's phone number and the message content are processed by Twilio.

### 11.4 Monitoring
- **Sentry** — application error and diagnostics tracking (Section 9).

A current, itemised list of sub-processors — including the nature of processing and hosting locations — is maintained for Schools and made available under the DPA. We will inform Schools of intended changes to sub-processors as provided in the DPA so they may object.

We may also disclose data where required to comply with law, valid legal process, or a lawful request by a public authority; to enforce our agreements; or to protect the rights, safety, and security of users, the public, or ArkenEdu — and, in the event of a corporate reorganisation, merger, or acquisition, subject to the protections of this Policy and the DPA.

## 13. Data retention

- **While the service is active.** We retain School data for as long as the School maintains an active relationship with ArkenEdu and as needed to provide the Platform. Academic records are **stamped to an academic year** and are designed to be retained across years (including the carry-over of arrears and the promotion of students between years) so Schools can maintain longitudinal records.
- **On the School's instruction.** Schools control retention of their data and may instruct us to correct, export, or delete records, subject to the DPA and applicable law.
- **After termination.** Following termination of a School's subscription, we retain School data for a limited wind-down period to allow export, and then delete or irreversibly anonymise it in accordance with the DPA, except where retention is required by law.
- **Operational data we control.** Logs, diagnostics, and backups are retained for limited, defined periods appropriate to their purpose (for example security and troubleshooting) and are then deleted or overwritten in the ordinary course.

## 14. Your rights

Subject to applicable law (including the DPDP Act and the SPDI Rules) and to the role of the School as Data Fiduciary, individuals whose personal data is processed through the Platform may have the right to:

- **access** the personal data held about them and obtain information about its processing;
- **correct, complete, or update** inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **erase** personal data where there is no longer a lawful basis to retain it;
- **withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent (this does not affect prior lawful processing);
- **nominate** another individual to exercise their rights in the event of death or incapacity, as provided under the DPDP Act; and
- **grievance redressal** — to raise a complaint and have it addressed (Section 17), and to escalate to the **Data Protection Board of India** where applicable.

**How to exercise these rights.** Because the School is the Data Fiduciary for almost all Platform data, most requests should be made to your **School**, which controls the records and can action them directly in the Platform. The School may use ArkenEdu's tools to fulfil the request. If you contact ArkenEdu directly about School data, we will refer or assist the School as a processor. For data where ArkenEdu is the Data Fiduciary (Section 5.4), contact us directly (Section 18).

We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request, and we may decline or limit requests where permitted by law (for example where action would adversely affect the rights of others or conflict with a legal obligation).

## 15. Deleting your account and data

Because accounts are provisioned by Schools, **account deletion is normally handled by your School**, which can deactivate or remove accounts and records directly in the Platform.

If you wish to request deletion of your account and associated personal data, you may:

1. ask your School administrator to delete your account and records; or
2. contact ArkenEdu at **privacy@arkenedu.com** with the subject "Account Deletion Request". We will verify the request, coordinate with the relevant School where ArkenEdu acts as processor, and action the deletion in accordance with Sections 13 and 14.

Certain data may be retained after deletion where required by law, to resolve disputes, to enforce agreements, or in routine backups that are cycled out on a defined schedule.

## 16. Cookies and similar technologies

The web application uses cookies and similar technologies that are **strictly necessary** to provide the service, including:

- **Authentication/session cookies** — HttpOnly, role-keyed cookies used to keep you signed in and to enforce role-based access (Section 7);

- **Preference storage** — used to remember basic interface settings.

We do **not** use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies. Because our cookies are strictly necessary for the Platform to function, disabling them in your browser will prevent you from signing in and using the service.
